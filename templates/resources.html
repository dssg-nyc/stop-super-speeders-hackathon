Understanding the critical importance of traffic safety and preventing speed-related fatalities

The Problem

Traffic violence is a leading cause of death in New York. Speeding is a major contributing factor to severe crashes and fatalities on our streets. Super speeders‚Äîdrivers with repeated speed violations‚Äî pose a significant risk to public safety.

The Solution

The SAFENY program uses data-driven detection to identify repeat speed offenders who meet specific thresholds. These drivers are required to install Intelligent Speed Assistance (ISA) devices that prevent their vehicles from exceeding posted speed limits.

Detection Criteria:

  • 16 or more speed camera tickets in 12 months, OR
  • 11 or more speed-related points in 18 months

Beta NYC

Beta NYC is a nonprofit organization that partners with the City of New York to design and test innovative solutions to public challenges. They focus on leveraging data, technology, and research to improve city services and outcomes.

Their work includes collaborating on traffic safety initiatives, analyzing datasets to identify patterns in violations, and developing evidence-based interventions that actually work. Beta NYC helps bridge the gap between data and policy implementation.

NYC Data Science for Social Good (NYC-DSSG)

NYC-DSSG is an initiative that brings together data scientists, technologists, and nonprofit leaders to tackle critical social challenges through data-driven approaches. They work on projects that directly impact vulnerable populations and community safety.

Their mission includes traffic safety, public health, and criminal justice. By combining advanced analytics with domain expertise, NYC-DSSG helps organizations use data responsibly to create measurable social impact.
